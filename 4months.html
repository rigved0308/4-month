Campal nights, Goa seas & waiting Aprils.

We started as “just friends” in Goa, but Campal, private beaches, guest houses and this month of waiting proved we’re way more than that. Scroll through the chapters, one month at a time, and then read the letter at the end.

december · chapter one
Campal, Goa – the night “more than friends” became real.

19–21 December at Campal. Streetlights, soft breeze, that familiar side of Goa – and you, slowly leaning in and resting your head on my shoulder. Our chit‑chat wasn’t dramatic, but everything inside me went quiet and loud at the same time. In that moment, it stopped feeling like “just friendship” and started feeling like “if I’m honest with myself, this is something serious.”

Since then, every Goa visit has its Campal checkpoint. We may go to a hundred other places, but somehow we end up there at least once, like we’re checking in with the place that first held our “us”.

campal · your head on my shoulder

I still remember the weight of your head, the warmth of your hand, the way you trusted my shoulder like it had always been yours. Even now, when I feel lost, I imagine that exact moment and my heart calms down.
february · chapter two
Private beach, Valentine’s Day & a chaotic first kiss.

February gave us that private beach – just the sound of waves, sand under our feet, and all our feelings catching up to us. We comforted each other there, talked about fears, distance, and how deep this thing between us had already become. We ended up celebrating Valentine’s Day in our own way – not with a perfect setup, but with real conversations and real comfort.

Our first kiss happened there too. Too many people around, too much disturbance, everything far from ideal. But when your lips met mine, all I could think was, “it’s her. after all this time, it’s actually her.” The chaos around us almost made it better – because even with all that noise, you still felt like the only thing in focus.

That kiss wasn’t just “finally”. It was my heart quietly signing a contract with you: even in noisy, messy, disturbed moments, you’re still the peace I want.
march · chapter three
Adilson Guest House – our first stay together.

March took us to Adilson Guest House. One room, two idiots in love, and a hundred small moments. Waking up next to you. Laughing over nothing. Seeing each other’s real moods – sleepy, cranky, soft, clingy, quiet. We had our first real physical moments there, nervous and emotional and honest. For me, it was never just about bodies. It was about trust, about choosing to be completely vulnerable with you, about knowing that my safest place in that room was always your arms.

That stay didn’t just bring us closer physically – it made me very sure that I want a life where seeing you in the same room is normal, not rare.

My favourite part wasn’t any “big” moment. It was you half‑asleep next to me, breathing softly, and my brain whispering, “if this is what home feels like, I want this forever.”
april · chapter four
A sky full of stars and a month full of waiting.

April has fewer photos, but the heaviest feelings. No Campal bench, no private beach, no Adilson room – just calls, chats and a heart that keeps saying “I wish she was here right now.”

Every day feels like a countdown to our next hug. I overthink routes, tickets, timings. I replay your laugh in my head, scroll through old pictures, and imagine running through Goa streets just to reach you faster. I miss you so much that sometimes it feels physical – like my chest is literally begging to be in your arms again. If I could, I’d drop everything, run to you, and hug you so tight that the distance and noise in our heads go completely silent for a while.

I keep telling myself: this waiting is temporary, but the way I feel about you isn’t. We’re not fighting for a weekend; we’re fighting for a whole lifetime of normal days together.
epilogue · a letter from me to you
You matter to me. This is only our beginning.
Hi love,

When I stitch it all together – Campal nights, our private beach, Adilson mornings, this starry April – the story is loud and clear:

you matter to me.

You’re the girl whose head on my shoulder turned Campal into our place. The girl I held on a noisy beach and still felt like the world blurred out. The girl I shared a room and my most vulnerable side with in Adilson. The girl I’m missing right now so much that my whole body wants to run and hug you until you feel every “I love you” I haven’t said yet.

This isn’t a small phase I’ll look back on someday as “that Goa story”. I don’t want that. I want this to be the beginning of the story that stays. This is just our beginning. I want us to make it till the end – in a good way. With better communication, softer fights, calmer hearts, more trust, more hugs, more “we’ll figure it out together.”

Whenever April feels too long or distance feels too loud, remember this: I’m not here halfway. I’m all in. My heart has already picked you, and it keeps picking you every day – in Campal, on beaches, in guest houses, and in all the boring, beautiful normal days we haven’t reached yet.

Four months down. A whole lifetime of scenes still waiting for us.

– your boy, who can’t wait to run to you and hug you so tight
